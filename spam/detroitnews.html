Thursday, October 22, 1998



What's new

Compiled by Detroit News Staff Writer Barbara Hoover.


    Gardening
    Guard your yard with imaginative art
    True, most of your garden crops are harvested by now, but that gives you more time to build an imaginative scarecrow. It makes great Halloween art, and will be ready to guard the yard next spring. For ideas, read Scarecrows: Making Harvest Figures and Other Yard Folks, by Felder Rushing (Storey Books, $19.95, 104 pages). The book roams way beyond the basic straw-stuffed shirt to such exotica as a leafy bride, a Tin Man made from drainpipe, and a skeleton in a gold lame jacket festooned with beads. The book is at bookstores, or by calling (800) 441-5700. Online, visit www.storey.com.
   
    Food
    Tasty verses honor maligned lunch meat
    John Nagamichi Cho, a researcher at Massachusetts Institute of Technology, is master of a legendary website: the SPAM haiku archives. Now, he's collected the best of his verses in a book: SPAM-ku: Tranquil Reflections on Luncheon Loaf (HarperPerennial, $7.95). Although he says many of the verses are not strictly haiku (they are "senryu"), they still are delicious: "Perfection uncanned/ Like a beautiful redhead/ Fresh from her trailer," or, "Four children later/ We run out of things to say. 'Price of SPAM is up.' " web site for Spam haikus:www.pemtropics.mit.edu/~jcho/spam/
   
    Kids
    Get snapped for Halloween
    If you'd like a more polished photo of your little ballerina or astronaut this Halloween than you're likely to snap yourself, Sears Portrait Studios will do one for free through Oct. 31. Children must be age 13 or younger and be accompanied by an adult. The child can show up in costume or change at the studio. Only one pose will be shot. You'll get the finished photo in about two weeks. If you want more poses, or an "instant" portrait to take with you, they're available at Sears' usual prices.
