Aspect Sensitivity and Orientation of Polar Summer Mesospheric Radar Scattering Layers: A Case Study

We introduce an optimization procedure for aspect sensitivity calculations for Doppler-beam-swinging (DBS) radar data. Furthermore, we define the tropogram, a measure of the backscatter anisotropy, and the clinogram, a vector field plot of the tilt directions of aspect-sensitive scattering layers. We apply these methods to polar mesospheric summer echo data and demonstrate their effectiveness and utility. We conclude that even with the optimization procedure extraction of unambiguous aspect sensitivity information is problematic for DBS systems, especially for those with broad oblique beams. On the other hand, the clinogram holds promise as a further aid in the study of wave dynamics using DBS radar data.
